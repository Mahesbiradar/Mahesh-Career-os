# INTERVIEW ENGINE
Version: 1.0

---

# PURPOSE

The Interview Engine transforms every chapter of the Knowledge Base into interview-ready learning material.

Its purpose is not to test memory.

Its purpose is to prepare the learner for real Software Engineering interviews.

Every chapter should help the learner understand

• what interviewers ask,

• why they ask it,

• how to answer,

• and where the concept is used in real engineering.

---

# PRIMARY OBJECTIVE

Bridge the gap between

Learning

↓

Interview Readiness

↓

Real Engineering

Every chapter should prepare the learner for all three.

---

# INTERVIEW PHILOSOPHY

Interview preparation is not memorization.

Interview preparation is understanding.

The learner should understand

• the concept

• the reason

• the trade-offs

• the practical usage

• the engineering decisions

Questions should encourage thinking.

Not memorization.

---

# RESPONSIBILITIES

The Interview Engine is responsible for

• Interview Questions

• Interview Traps

• Scenario Questions

• Practical Thinking

• Engineering Discussions

• Revision Preparation

---

# QUESTION GENERATION

Every chapter should contain

Basic Questions

Intermediate Questions

Scenario Based Questions

Avoid generating a large number of questions.

Quality is more important than quantity.

---

# BASIC QUESTIONS

Purpose

Check conceptual understanding.

Examples

What is a Dictionary?

What is a Process?

What is an Entity?

What is HTTP?

These should be answerable in under one minute.

---

# INTERMEDIATE QUESTIONS

Purpose

Test deeper understanding.

Examples

Why use dict.get() instead of []?

Difference between Process and Thread?

Why use Transactions?

When should normalization stop?

These questions require reasoning.

---

# SCENARIO QUESTIONS

Purpose

Simulate real interviews.

Every chapter should contain scenario-based thinking whenever possible.

Examples

Python

An API receives "age":"25".

How should it be validated?

---

Operating Systems

A process is waiting for disk I/O.

Why is CPU utilization low?

---

DBMS

A shopping application has duplicate customer records.

How could better database design prevent this?

---

Backend

JWT token expires.

What should the API return?

Scenario questions should connect learning with engineering.

---

# DO NOT GENERATE

Do not generate

Puzzle questions

Trick questions

Language trivia

Very advanced questions for beginner topics

Questions unrelated to today's topic

Keep interview preparation aligned with the learner's level.

---

# QUALITY RULES

Questions should

Encourage understanding

Promote discussion

Reflect real interviews

Connect with engineering

Improve confidence

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

---

# FAILURE CONDITIONS

The Interview Engine has failed if

• Questions are generic.

• Questions test only memorization.

• No scenario questions exist.

• Interview traps are missing.

• Backend relevance is ignored.

• Revision section is weak.

---

# SUCCESS CONDITIONS

The Interview Engine succeeds when

The learner understands

What interviewers ask.

Why they ask it.

How to answer confidently.

How to apply the concept in real projects.

The learner should feel prepared for both technical interviews and practical engineering discussions.

---

# FINAL PRINCIPLE

Interview preparation is not about collecting questions.

It is about developing engineering thinking.

Every interview section should make the learner a better Software Engineer, not just a better interview candidate.
